Nigeria Regions

Northern Nigeria

Northern Nigeria is a very dry place, and its only gotten worse. Much of Northern Nigeria is high and flat planes with a lot of trees.

Northern Nigeria has 2 main ethnic groups, the Hausa and the Fulani. The original people were the Hausa. More than 1/3 of the population speak Hausa, while the Fulani speak the less common Fulfulde.

Western Nigeria

Western Nigeria is a very crowded place with "go-slows" (traffic jams) regularly happening.

When people first arrived in Western Nigeria, a forest had developed along the coastline, but when the people arrived, they cut it down to make more farmland and to sell wood for whatever the people may need.

Western Nigeria is dominated by the Yoruba, who arrived at around 100 B.C.E

Eastern Nigeria

Eastern Nigeria is along the Gulf of Guinea. This region receives a lot more rainfall because it was originally a rain forest. Now, its mostly swamps along the coast.

The predominant culture of Eastern Nigeria is the Igbo (pronounced as "ee-bo"). Before the british colonial rule, there were many igbo dialects spoken, now there isn't unfortunately.
